# Key Findings and Recommendation

1. Total Customer Base and Churn

    The dataset includes 6,311 total customers.
    Out of these, 1,705 customers have churned, and hence the overall churn rate is 27.02%.
    This indicates that more than one in four customers are leaving, making churn a critical business issue.

#

2. Contract Type & Churn Behavior

    Customers on month-to-month contracts have a significantly higher probability of churning.
    Customers who have Two-Year contracts have the lowest churn rate, reflecting customer loyalty.
    
    * Insight:
    Long-term contracts also play a big role in increasing customer retention.
    
    * Actionable Recommendation:
    retention strategies would be directed at the Month-to-Month customers through discounts, loyalty benefits, and contract upgrades.

#

3. Early Churn (First 6 Months)

    276 customers churned within the first 6 months of joining.
    This also highlights the issues related to dissatisfaction or ineffective onboarding during the early period.
    
    * Insight:
    The initial 6 months are a critical period in the customer lifecycle.
    
    * Recommendation: Improve onboarding, proactive support, and engaging the early adopters. 

# 

4. Internet Type & Churn
   
     Customers using the Fiber Optic internet services face a higher churn rate when compared with other internet services.
     Other possible contributing factors may include pricing concerns, service quality issues, or enhanced offerings by competitors.

#

5. Top Reasons for Customer Churn

    The most common causes of churn are:
    
    * Competitors with better devices
    * Competitors with more data at lower price
    * Poor customer support attitude or experience
    
    * Insight:
    The main drivers of customer churn do not include pricing; they include experience of services and competitive intensity.

#

6. Churn Categories

    Competitor-related and Attitude-related churn types have the highest volume of churn.
    
    * This suggests:
    Gaps in customer service quality
    Need for stronger competitive positioning

#

7. Age-Based Churn Patterns

    Senior customers, categorized as those aged 65 and above, have a larger population and the highest churn rate.
    Younger customer segments show lower churn levels, implying improved adaptability and satisfaction levels.
    
    * Insight:
    Senior customers might have issues relating to usability, service intricacy, and expectations. 

#

Business Summary (For Stakeholders) 

Churn analysis results have shown that Month-to-Month contracts, strong competitor offerings, customer service concerns, and high senior customer churn volume, especially fiber optic users, are the focal areas for customer churn concerns.
